How this works. The NEPI rates neighbourhoods on two energy costs — Form (building energy) and Mobility (transport energy) — predicted from planner-controllable inputs. The primary slider moves all inputs together along the observed national gradient from sprawling detached suburbs (Band E, ~25,000 kWh/hh/yr) to compact urban flats (Band B, ~16,000 kWh/hh/yr). Individual overrides let you test targeted interventions.

Four XGBoost models with monotonic constraints predict Form energy, Mobility energy, car ownership, and commute distance from 198,779 English Output Areas. Each model sees only its causally relevant features: building era affects Form but not Mobility; transit access affects Mobility but not Form.
